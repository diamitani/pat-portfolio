Prospect Automation Engine

A daily intent-to-outreach pipeline that turns yesterday's buying signals into routed, enriched, ICP-clean accounts — automatically.

Daily intent → routed, deduped, ICP-clean accounts with zero manual triage

Overview

Every morning, this n8n workflow pulls the companies that showed buying intent yesterday (firmographic + ABM intent signals) from the CRM, runs them through a gauntlet of ICP and safety filters — under 2,000 employees, no existing customers, no open deals, no competitors, no schools/governments, no out-of-region accounts, no recently-touched accounts — assigns each survivor to the right sales pod by region via a round-robin engine, and fires them into a data-enrichment table for contact discovery. The result: reps start each day with a fresh, deduped, territory-routed list of in-market accounts, with zero manual triage.

The Problem

Buying-intent signals were pouring into the CRM from third-party data providers, but converting them into actionable outbound meant a human manually filtering out customers, open deals, competitors, out-of-ICP accounts, and wrong regions every day — then hand-assigning each account to the right rep. Slow, inconsistent, and it meant hot intent went cold before anyone acted.

How It Works

  1. Daily schedule trigger — fires each morning in n8n, a workflow-automation platform that runs the pipeline unattended.
  2. Yesterday-window intent pull — a CRM company-search API call pulls companies whose intent-workflow date falls in yesterday's window, requesting ~30 firmographic + intent properties.
  3. Split + size gate — each company is split out and filtered to under 2,000 employees (ICP size).
  4. Out-of-region exclusion — a hand-written JS filter removes out-of-region (APAC) accounts using a multi-signal check: location fields, country codes, domain TLD, and company-name keywords — so an account with empty location fields is still caught by its domain.
  5. Safety gauntlet — sequential filters strip out companies with associated deals, customer lifecycle, customer type, and no recent sales activity (touched in the last 30 days), then a competitor filter removes named competitors.
  6. ICP filter — a JS node removes schools, universities, and government entities (keeping NGOs, which are valid prospects).
  7. Round-robin routing — a JS engine normalizes state/country and assigns each account to the correct regional pod, stamping the CRM owner ID, rep name, and email.
  8. Enrichment handoff — survivors are POSTed (batched) to a data-enrichment webhook for contact discovery, and upserted into an n8n Data Table as the run's source-of-truth record.

Setup Process

  1. Confirm the CRM intent properties exist (intent-workflow dates, firmographics) and are populated by your intent providers.
  2. Import the workflow JSON; bind the CRM credential and set the enrichment webhook URL.
  3. Edit the round-robin pod map (rep names, CRM owner IDs, emails) and the territory state sets to your team.
  4. Tune the ICP gates: employee cap, competitor names, excluded regions, and the recent-activity window.
  5. Dry-run against yesterday's window; verify the filter funnel counts and that routing assigns the right pod per region.
  6. Activate the daily schedule and confirm the Data Table logs each run.

Results

  • Live in production, running daily on the sales pipeline.
  • Turns raw daily intent into a routed, deduped, ICP-clean account list with zero manual triage.
  • Multi-signal out-of-region exclusion catches accounts even when location fields are blank (via domain TLD + name keywords).
  • Compliance-first by design: customers, open deals, competitors, and recently-touched accounts are all stripped before any account reaches a rep.

Challenges & Fixes

  • Out-of-region accounts slipping through on empty location fields → layered the filter with domain-TLD and company-name keyword detection, not just location fields.
  • Re-touching accounts already in play → sequential gates on associated deals, customer lifecycle/type, and 30-day recent-activity before routing.
  • Wasting enrichment credit on non-ICP orgs → schools/governments and 2,000+ employee companies filtered before the enrichment handoff.
  • Uneven rep distribution → a normalized state/country → pod round-robin so territory assignment is deterministic and auditable.
