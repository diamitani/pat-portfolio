The Challenge

Hot intent went cold in the queue.

Buying-intent signals were pouring into the CRM from third-party data providers — ZoomInfo firmographics, Factors.ai ABM signals — but converting them into actionable outbound meant a human manually filtering out customers, open deals, competitors, out-of-ICP accounts, and wrong regions every single day, then hand-assigning each account to the right rep.

Slow, inconsistent, and worst of all: hot intent went cold before anyone acted. An account researching EOR services yesterday needed to be in a rep's queue this morning — not whenever someone got around to the spreadsheet.

The approach: make the filters structural

Every exclusion a human used to apply by eye became a deterministic gate in the pipeline: size, region, lifecycle, open deals, competitors, entity type, recent touches. Accounts that survive get routed by a round-robin engine and handed to enrichment automatically. If it reaches a rep, it's already clean — compliance-first by design, not by review.

The full architecture (v2) chains the whole motion: intent sources (ZoomInfo live, Factors.ai staged, inbound planned) → HubSpot CRM → the 7 AM n8n filter chain and round-robin owner assignment → Clay enrichment with a CEO-fallback contact path → an LLM decision layer → Amplemarket outreach. Every stage observable, every run logged.

What Was Built

A morning gauntlet, run unattended.

Each morning the workflow pulls every company that showed buying intent yesterday and marches it through the gates.

01 · Trigger + Pull

Yesterday-window intent pull

A daily schedule fires the workflow; a CRM company-search call pulls every company whose intent-workflow date falls in yesterday's window, with ~30 firmographic and intent properties.

02 · Size Gate

ICP sizing

Companies are split out and filtered to under 2,000 employees — the ICP ceiling — before anything else spends time or money on them.

03 · Region Filter

Multi-signal exclusion

A hand-written JS filter removes out-of-region accounts using location fields, country codes, domain TLD, and company-name keywords — so an account with empty location fields is still caught by its domain.

04 · Safety Gauntlet

Never re-touch what's in play

Sequential filters strip companies with associated deals, customer lifecycle or type, and any sales activity in the last 30 days; a competitor filter removes named competitors, and an ICP filter drops schools and governments (keeping NGOs).

05 · Routing

Round-robin territory engine

A JS engine normalizes state and country, assigns each survivor to the correct regional pod, and stamps the CRM owner ID, rep name, and email — territory assignment is deterministic, not tribal.

06 · Handoff

Enrichment + source of truth

Survivors are POSTed in batches to a Clay enrichment webhook for contact discovery and upserted into an n8n Data Table as the run's auditable record.

Challenges & Fixes

Where it broke, and how it stopped breaking.

  • Out-of-region accounts slipping through on empty location fields → the filter was layered with domain-TLD and company-name keyword detection, not just location fields — absence of data is not evidence of eligibility.
  • Re-touching accounts already in play → sequential gates on associated deals, customer lifecycle and type, and a 30-day recent-activity window run before routing, so a rep never collides with a live conversation.
  • Wasting enrichment credit on non-ICP orgs → schools, governments, and 2,000+ employee companies are filtered before the enrichment handoff — the same cost-engineering rule as every system in the library: free checks run before paid steps.
  • Uneven rep distribution → a normalized state/country → pod round-robin makes territory assignment deterministic and balanced instead of first-come-first-grabbed.
